Projects:

Iris Project

Description:

For my final project at Saskatchewan Polytechnic, I decided to build a weather balloon that measured the Pfozer maximum.

Typically speaking, this is something that space agencies will need to calculate when sending a rocket into space. The Pfozer maximum is the maximum altitude that a rocket can reach before the ozone layer becomes too thin to protect the rocket from the sun's radiation.

For my attempt, I designed quite a bit, from the microcontroller firmware, to custom PCBs, to the camera gymbal and software, and even to the balloon's hardware structure itself. Through some extensive testing I was able to integrate a geiger counter, altimeter, and a few other sensors to the microcontroller, connect this to a cable that ran through the structures frame and acted as a long-range antenna, and transmit this information in real-time back to a custom built android app. Just to add some extra flair, I also added a camera and a cutsom designed gymbal that would take a constant video for retrieval after the apparatus landed.

While I had many struggles throughout my capstone project, ultimately I was able to create something I was truly proud of and ended up getting a 99% on this project cementing my 4.0 GPA and admissions into the Deans Honor list for graduation.

Key Responsibillities: Android App Development | Firmware Development | PCB Design | RF Design | Project Management | Quality Assurance | Documentation | Compliance and Regulatory Considerations | Risk Assessment and Mitigation

Project Outcome: The project successfully demonstrated the design, construction, and operation of a sophisticated weather balloon system capable of measuring the Pfozer maximum, achieving real-time data transmission, and capturing continuous video footage, culminating in exceptional academic recognition and a 99% project evaluation score.
